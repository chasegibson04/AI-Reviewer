Abstract
We investigate the impact of quantum capacitance in carbon nanotube (CNT) structures. We find that the density of states plays a crucial role in the overall performance of nanoscale field-effect transistors.

Introduction
Nanoscale devices such as CNTs have shown immense promise [1, 2]. However, the classical capacitance limit is often challenged by the quantum capacitance (CQ) effect. As discussed by Smith et al., 2020, the low density of states in graphene-like materials leads to a non-negligible CQ contribution.

Methods
We fabricated single-walled CNTs using chemical vapor deposition. Measurements were taken using a custom probe station at room temperature. We did not use a negative control for the temperature variation.
