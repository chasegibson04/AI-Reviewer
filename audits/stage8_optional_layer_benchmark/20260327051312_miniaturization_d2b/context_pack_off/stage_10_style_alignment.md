# Stage 10 Style Alignment

## style_issues
- Inconsistent use of italics for chemical terms and abbreviations.
- Inconsistent use of bold formatting for figure and table labels.
- Inconsistent use of parentheses for chemical structures and compound numbers.
- Inconsistent use of abbreviations (e.g., 'r.t.' for 'room temperature' is sometimes written in full).
- Inconsistent use of punctuation in chemical structures and compound names.

## formatting_issues
- Inconsistent indentation and alignment of chemical structures and reaction schemes.
- Inconsistent use of line breaks and spacing between paragraphs and sections.
- Inconsistent use of bullet points and numbered lists.
- Inconsistent use of quotation marks for figure and table captions.
- Inconsistent use of hyphens and en dashes in compound names and reaction conditions.

## tone_issues
- The tone is generally formal and technical, which is appropriate for a scientific article, but some sentences could be more concise and direct.

## grammar_issues
- Some sentences are overly complex and could be simplified for clarity.
- Some sentences contain awkward phrasing or word choices.
- Some sentences are missing punctuation or have incorrect punctuation.

## structure_issues
- The introduction could be more clearly separated from the main body of the text.
- The transitions between sections could be smoother.
- The conclusions could be more clearly stated and summarized.

## clarity_issues
- Some technical terms and abbreviations are not clearly defined or explained.
- Some sentences are difficult to understand due to their complexity or lack of context.
- Some figures and tables are not clearly labeled or explained.
