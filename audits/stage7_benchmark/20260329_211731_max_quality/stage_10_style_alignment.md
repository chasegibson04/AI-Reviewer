# Stage 10 Style Alignment

## style_issues
- Repetitive phrasing: Phrases like 'reaction array' and 'ChatGPT' are used excessively.
- Wordiness: Sentences could be more concise. For example, 'This workflow is experimentally demonstrated, with modest to excellent yields of products obtained in each instance on the first attempt' could be simplified.
- Informal language: Phrases like 'seemingly' and 'to our knowledge' are not typical of scientific writing.
- Use of contractions: Contractions like 'doesn't' and 'can't' should be avoided in formal scientific writing.
- Redundancy: Statements like 'We emphasize that it is not advised...' are often unnecessary.
- Overuse of passive voice: While the guide requests active voice, there are instances of passive voice throughout the text.

## formatting_issues
- Inconsistent section headings: Some headings have brackets (e.g., '[INTRODUCTION]'), while others do not.
- Image references: Frequent mentions of 'intentionally omitted' images disrupt the flow and suggest incomplete formatting.
- Figure captions: Captions are lengthy and could be more concise. The repetition of information about color bars is redundant.
- DOI/citation inconsistencies: The text notes discrepancies between DOIs and article titles, indicating a formatting error.
- Bullet point formatting: Inconsistent indentation and spacing in bulleted lists.
- Use of bolding: Excessive use of bolding, particularly in figure captions, makes the text visually cluttered.

## tone_issues
- Overly enthusiastic language: Phrases like 'led to successful reaction conditions in every case interrogated' sound promotional rather than objective.
- Self-promotion: The authors repeatedly highlight the benefits of their workflow and software (phactor).
- Critical tone towards ChatGPT: While acknowledging its limitations, the authors frequently point out ChatGPT's errors (e.g., hallucinated citations, missing water additive).
- Lack of objectivity: The presentation of results is biased towards showcasing the success of the ChatGPT-phactor workflow.

## alignment_actions
- Revise for conciseness: Eliminate redundant phrases and simplify complex sentences.
- Standardize section headings: Ensure consistent formatting for all headings.
- Address image references: Either include the images or remove the references.
- Refine figure captions: Make captions more concise and informative.
- Verify DOIs and citations: Ensure accuracy and consistency of all references.
- Adjust tone: Adopt a more objective and neutral tone, minimizing self-promotion and critical language.
- Ensure consistent voice: Prioritize active voice throughout the manuscript.
- Review for wordiness: Scrutinize each sentence for unnecessary words and phrases.
- Check for contractions: Replace all contractions with their full forms.
- Adhere to word limit: Ensure the manuscript does not exceed 2200 words.
