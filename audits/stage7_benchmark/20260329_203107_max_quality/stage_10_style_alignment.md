# Stage 10 Style Alignment

## style_issues
- Repetitive phrasing: Phrases like 'reaction array' and 'ChatGPT' are used excessively.
- Wordiness: Sentences could be more concise. For example, 'This workflow is experimentally demonstrated, with modest to excellent yields of products obtained in each instance on the first attempt' could be simplified.
- Informal language: Phrases like 'seemingly' and 'to our knowledge' are not typical of scientific writing.
- Use of contractions: Contractions like 'doesn’t' and 'can’t' should be avoided in formal scientific writing.
- Redundancy: Statements like 'We emphasize that it is not advised to ask the model for archival data such as DOIs or direct citations at this time' are somewhat redundant given the earlier observation of hallucinated citations.

## formatting_issues
- Inconsistent section headings: Some section headings have brackets (e.g., '[INTRODUCTION]'), while others do not.
- Image references: Frequent mentions of 'intentionally omitted' images disrupt the flow and suggest incomplete formatting.
- Figure captions: Captions are lengthy and could be more concise. The repetition of information about 'color bars' is unnecessary.
- DOI/citation inconsistencies: The text notes discrepancies between DOIs and article titles, indicating a formatting or data entry error.
- Bullet point formatting: Inconsistent use of dashes (-) versus bullet points for lists.
- Inconsistent use of bolding: Bold text is used for emphasis, but the application is not always consistent.

## tone_issues
- Overly enthusiastic language: Phrases like 'led to successful reaction conditions in every case interrogated' sound promotional rather than objective.
- Self-promotion: The frequent highlighting of phactor's capabilities feels like marketing.
- Critical tone towards ChatGPT: While acknowledging limitations, the text often emphasizes ChatGPT's failures (e.g., hallucinated citations, missing water additive) more than its successes.
- Lack of objectivity: The authors' excitement about the workflow occasionally overshadows a neutral presentation of the results.

## alignment_actions
- Revise for conciseness: Eliminate redundant phrases and simplify complex sentences.
- Standardize formatting: Ensure consistent section headings, bullet point styles, and bolding.
- Address image issues: Either include the images or remove the references to them.
- Verify DOIs and citations: Correct any discrepancies in the reference list.
- Adopt a more neutral tone: Present the results objectively, avoiding overly enthusiastic language and self-promotion.
- Refine figure captions: Make captions more concise and informative.
- Adhere to style guide: Ensure the manuscript follows the lab's style guide regarding language, voice, and limitations.
